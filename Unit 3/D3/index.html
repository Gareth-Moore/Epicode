My Favourite Album

Ashes of the Wake - Lamb of God

   "Ashes of the Wake is the fourth studio album and first major-label release by American heavy metal band Lamb of God, released in 2004 via Epic Records. The album was inspired by the events that took place during the war in Iraq with songs such as "Ashes of the Wake" (which includes snippets of former Marine Staff Sergeant Jimmy Massey in an interview after his return from the Iraq War)"

My Thoughts:
   From top to bottom Ashes of the Wake is a hurtling beast of an album. My favourite jams from this list are Laid to Rest, a heart thumping fist pumping sprint through chaos, and Blood Of The Scribe which invokes a sense of swelling heat and frustration soothed only by the incredible work of Chris Adler (drummer) before the hammer falls, the tension snaps and you're left with adrenalin coursing through your veins.

Hall of Shame

Open if you dare...

Yellow by Coldplay

Nails on a chalk board, that inconsiderate person slurping coffee or someone who chews with their mouth open... this song is like that.

I don't feel like dancing by Scissor Sisters

Oh gosh where do I start with this one? Imagine the wet dog smell, a rogue pixel on your screen, tangled earphone wires, and being stuck in bumper-to-bumper traffic in song form.

Friday by Rebecca Black

Friday usually evokes joy and a feeling of accomplishment. So it is odd that this 'song' is called Friday because it feels like a Monday. A universally despised contraption of frustration.
